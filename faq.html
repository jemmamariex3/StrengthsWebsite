FAQ

Please review the following questions and answers for our website.

Thank you!

 

  • What is the Strengths Assessment?

    The Strengths assessment is a personality survey that presents you with 177 questions, and using Positive Psychology, endeavors to determine your strongest areas of natural talent. In order to foster honesty and instinct, you are given only twenty seconds to answer each question.

     

    You will choose between a pair of descriptors; for example: “I prefer to have a plan” versus “I go with the flow.” In deciding, you will be able to assign a range to designate to what extent each descriptor applies to you. When complete, the results are calculated and you are provided with a list of your Top 5 Strengths Themes.

     

    Learning about these themes is the first step toward personal, academic, and professional excellence. Please allow thirty to forty-five minutes for a thorough and successful assessment.

     

  • What is a Strength?

    A Strength is the ability to consistently provide near-perfect performance in a particular activity. The key to building a Strength is to identify your dominant talents and enhance them by acquiring knowledge and skills pertinent to that activity.

     

    Strengths are the product of investing skill, knowledge, and practice into your unique natural abilities. Such conscious development allows you to use your Strengths as polished tools to achieve consistently successful performances.

     

  • What is Positive Psychology?

    Positive Psychology is the study of the strengths that enable individuals and communities to thrive. It is a paradigm that encompasses an approach to psychology from the perspective of healthy, successful life functioning. By focusing on the positive aspects of experiences, traits, and life events, a person can build resilience and “grit” (perseverance.)

     

    The field is founded on the idea that people desire to lead fulfilling and purposeful lives, to nourish the best parts of themselves, and to enhance their experiences of work, play, and love. The goal is to develop a sense of optimism towards your life that combats hopelessness and encourages happiness and well-being.

     

  • What is a talent?

    A talent is a naturally recurring pattern of thought, feeling, or behavior that can be productively applied. Talents are the natural tendencies that give you the aptitude to do something well.

     

    These unique patterns are innately within you, and identifying a talent helps you to apply the knowledge and skill that will take something instinctual and polish it into something extraordinary.

     

     

  • What is the difference between

    a Strength and a talent?

    Talents are the raw materials required to develop Strengths. Talents are naturally and essentially within you, and when talents are cultivated, they will develop into Strengths.

     

  • What is the difference between

    a Strength and a skill?

    Skills are hard-earned areas of expertise that have been formed over long periods of practice and application. A Strength is a naturally present area of talent that has been nourished and developed to excellence.

     

     

  • How does it benefit me to know

     my Strengths?

    Discovering and understanding your Strengths will be a tool with endless value. It will begin with personal development, creating a sense of confidence and surety of self that will buoy everything that you do, and turn you into a natural leader. Of course, this inevitably leads to academic and professional improvement, as well.

     

    You will discover strategies, using your Strengths, that will advance your study skills and enliven your campus life. You will also learn the best ways to conduct yourself during interviews, how to make your resume shine, and of course, uncover the best jobs for utilizing your Strengths.

     

     

     

  • How do I apply my Strengths

    to my everyday life?

    Knowing your Strengths can improve everything from your relationships to your career to your academic life. The journey of self-discovery creates confidence and awareness that fuel everything that you do.

     

    Going out into the world with a firm sense of self and value will draw people to you and generate success in all areas of your life. You will understand the best ways to function in order to be at your best, and this enlightenment will enhance every situation.

     

     

     

     

  • Why does the assessment only reveal

     my Top 5 Strengths? Can I discover all 34?

    The purpose of the assessment is to help you discover your most dominant talents, so that you may start along your path to success by focusing on those that are prevalent. The more prominent the talents, the greater the opportunity for development.

  • If I take the assessment a second time,

    will my results be different?

    Your essence and your talents – how you naturally feel, think, and behave – are natural within you, and they are unlikely to change significantly over the course of your life. Personality research reveals that your talents are remarkably consistent by the time you become an adult.

     

    If you have dominant Woo talents now, in all likelihood, they will still be prevalent when you’re eighty years old! Does this mean that you won’t change at all throughout your life? Of course not! You will surely change in some aspects. But as your life progresses, you will more fully develop who you truly are.

     

  • What if I don’t feel like my Top

    5 Strengths are true for me?

    Sometimes, a person may feel as though one of their Top 5 Strengths is not particularly fitting, or they don’t feel that it resonates with their idea of themselves. In cases like this, we recommend taking some time to truly get to know the Strength in question.

     

    Research the typical qualities of someone with this Strength, and locate others who have mastered this Strength, in order to see in what ways it might apply to you. Discover the kinds of jobs and extracurricular activities that are suitable for this particular Strength, and contemplate why someone might excel in these areas.

     

    Strengths manifest in many different ways. If you don’t feel that a particular Strength definition is relevant to you, it only means that your Strength operates in a different or unique way. It is exciting to think about the special ways in which you can utilize your specific Strength!

  • Can my Strengths point me

    towards the right career path?

    The assessment was designed for use as a developmental tool. Although certain Strengths are quite consistently dominant within certain professions, your top 5 themes, ultimately, should not dictate your life path. Instead, your themes should help you to excel wherever you happen to be in life or career. Knowing your Strengths can guide you in determining how to best capitalize on your talents, in whatever role or profession you choose.

     

  • Will I be “labeled” or “put in a box”

    based on my Strengths?

    Not at all. Instead, your Strengths will be a tool to help you along the path of self-discovery. They are intended to raise your natural tendencies and potentials. The Strengths approach was created to help you learn and understand how to apply your own specific talents in all areas of your life, to enhance your confidence and your probability of success.

  • How likely is it to have the same Top 5 Strengths as somebody else?

    It is important to remember that your five unique Strengths, in their unique order, truly make you the individual that you are. The chances of having the exact same Top 5 Strengths in the exact same order as someone else is 1 in 33.39 million!

     

    The chances of having the same Top 5 Strengths as someone else, in any order, is approximately 1 in 340,000. These numbers are proof that only you can bring your particular gifts to the table. Only you can apply those particular five Strengths together in unison and absolutely shine!

     

  • Should I just ignore my weaknesses,

    now that I know my Strengths?

    Learning your Strengths is not about ignoring weaknesses, but instead, it aims to cultivate areas in which you are already strong, so that you can use your Strengths to manage your weaknesses.

     

    Determining the best ways in which you function can help to overcome areas in which you struggle, so that your weaknesses never hold you back from success.

  • What is the reliability and validity

    of the Strengths assessment?

    The assessment of Strengths has been utilized for over thirty years, and millions of people just like you have been tested and polled to ensure reliable results. Extensive data has been collected and many years of research have been applied, in order to create an efficient and dependable algorithm for measuring results.

     

  • Are there accommodations for people

    with disabilities to take the assessment?

    Yes! There is a 20-second timer for each pair of statements on the assessment. However, it can be turned off if you have a disability that requires you to participate without the time limit. Visit the Career Center for more information regarding visually impaired students.

     

  • Is the Strengths assessment

    available in other languages?

    Yes! The assessment is offered in more than twenty languages! There will be an option to choose your language before beginning your assessment.
